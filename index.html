💾 NAS Storage Explained

A NAS (Network Attached Storage) is a dedicated storage device connected to your network. It allows multiple computers, phones, and smart devices to access files from one central location.

🏠 Home NAS

Capacity:
4TB - 40TB

Best for:

  • Family photo backups
  • Movies and music servers
  • Personal cloud storage

⚙️ Advanced NAS

Capacity:
40TB - 200TB+

Best for:

  • Large media libraries
  • Small businesses
  • Multiple users

🏢 Enterprise NAS

Capacity:
Hundreds of TB to Petabytes

Best for:

  • Companies
  • Research facilities
  • Large archives

RAID Type Purpose

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the fastest storage available?

Modern NVMe SSDs are among the fastest consumer storage devices, reaching over 10,000 MB/s in some models.

What is the longest lasting storage?

Currently available options include archival optical storage such as M-DISC and specialized archival systems. Experimental technologies like quartz crystal and DNA storage may last thousands of years but are not widely available.

Is NAS better than cloud storage?

NAS gives you physical control and large local capacity. Cloud storage provides remote access and easier off-site backup.

How much storage does the human brain have?

Some estimates compare the brain to approximately petabyte-scale storage, although the comparison is not a direct equivalent to computer memory.
