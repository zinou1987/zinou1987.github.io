NAS Storage Explained

NAS (Network Attached Storage) is a personal or professional storage system connected to a network. It allows multiple devices to access files, backups, movies, photos, and documents.

🏠 Home NAS

Typical capacity:
4TB-40TB

Best for:
Photos, videos, backups, personal media servers

⚙️ Advanced NAS

Typical capacity:
40TB-200TB+

Best for:
Power users and small businesses

🏢 Enterprise NAS

Capacity:
Hundreds of TB to Petabytes

Used by:
Companies and data centers

Storage Units Explained

Understanding storage sizes helps compare how much information different technologies can hold.

Unit Size Example
Kilobyte (KB) 1,000 bytes Small text files
Megabyte (MB) 1,000 KB Songs, images, ebooks
Gigabyte (GB) 1,000 MB Phone storage, games, movies
Terabyte (TB) 1,000 GB Hard drives and large media libraries
Petabyte (PB) 1,000 TB Large servers and data centers
Exabyte (EB) 1,000 PB Global-scale information systems

1GB

Hundreds of ebooks or thousands of photos.

1TB

Hundreds of HD movies or thousands of large files.

1PB

Millions of photos and massive archives.
